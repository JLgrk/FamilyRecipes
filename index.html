Pierogi

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 Tablespoons melted butter

Filling

  • 1 lb. farmers cheese (dry kind)
  • 1 egg
  • 3 medium potatoes (cooked with 1/4 t. salt)
  • 1/2 medium onion grated
  • 2 Tablespoons butter

Directions

  1. Mix all dough ingredients and knead in a bowl then on a floured board until smooth. Cover with bowl and let rest for 1/2 hour.
  2. For filling: Mix well the cheese and egg and set aside.
  3. Fry grated onion with butter until onions are soft.
  4. Mash cooked potatoes with fried onions.

  5. Cut dough into fourths and roll 1/4 on a floured board to 1/8 inch thickness.
  6. Cut circle with dough press and fill with approximately 1 tablespoon of filling.
  7. Press edges together, apply water to inside edge if edges will not stick.

  8. In a deep pot, add 1/2 teaspoon of salt to water.
  9. Drop pierogi gently into water, when pierogi float to top reduce heat and boil for 10 minutes.
  10. Remove with slotted spoon and place in a colander.
  11. Rinse lightly with cold water. Pierogi may be frozen after they cool.

Note: Recipe as written makes about twice as much filling as dough.

Kapusta

Ingredients

  • 14oz. can of saurkraut
  • 1 small cabbage or half of a medium
  • Pork fat back (a lot), cut very small
  • 3-4 yellow onions
  • 1 chicken bullion cube
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • Pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Fry the fat back.
  2. Chop onions and cabbage.
  3. Drain saurkraut and rinse with cold water.
  4. Put water and bullion in pot with cabbage.
  5. When cabbage starts to boil add saurkraut.
  6. Remove fat back from pan and add to cabbage mixture.
  7. Fry onions in fat back grease until soft.
  8. Add onions to main mixture.
  9. Cook main mixture slowly and covered until cabbage is done.
  10. Meanwhile make rue with fat back grease by adding flour and fry until brown.
  11. Add to main mixture after cabbage is done.

Polish Pancakes (Nalesniki)

Ingredients

  • 3 c. flour
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 4 tsp. sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 c. milk

Directions

  1. Sift together dry ingredients then add milk and eggs, batter should be thin.
  2. Pour on lightly greased griddle and flip when bottom starts to brown.
  3. While warm fill with applesauce, sweet cheese, or desired filling and roll up.

Nonny's Oriental Chicken Wings

Ingredients

Makes about 20 wings

  • 1 c. brown sugar
  • 3/4 c. cooking sherry
  • 1 tsp. dry mustard
  • 1 c. soy sauce

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  2. Place chicken wings in a single layer, skin side up, on a cookie sheet. Bake for 30-40 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile combine the other ingredients in a sauce pan and simmer over medium heat to dissolve sugar.
  4. Turn chicken wings over and bake for 10 minutes more.
  5. Place wings in a large deep pan and pour glaze over wings.
  6. Bake for 10 minutes, turn and bake for 10 minutes more.

Mom's Tomato Sauce

Ingredients

  • 2 Large cans of petite diced tomatoes
  • 2 cans tomato paste
  • 1 Large white or yellow onion, diced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic
  • Olive Oil
  • 3 Tablespoons Parsley
  • 4-5 Tablespoons Oregano
  • 1 Teaspoon Crushed Red Pepper flakes
  • Salt and Pepper
  • 3/4 cup Parmesan Cheese

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot and fry chopped onion until clear.
  2. Add crushed garlic to onions.
  3. Add tomatoes to pot, can be pureed first in food processor or diced tomatoes substituted with canned puree.
  4. Once heated add tomato paste, herbs and spices.
  5. Simmer on low for 2 hours stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  6. At 2 hours add the Parmesan cheese and continue to simmer for 2 more hours.
